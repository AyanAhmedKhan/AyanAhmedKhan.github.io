Research statement

A model that scores 100 % on the benchmark and 53 % in the field has not made an error in accuracy. It has made an error in confidence — and I have now found the same error in clinical tables, medical images, network traffic, demand forecasts and multilingual language models.

That recurrence is the point. The failure is not a property of medicine, or of vision, or of any one dataset — it is a property of how we evaluate. So the thing I actually build is evaluation machinery: leakage-free protocols, genuine external validation, calibration and conformal abstention, and honest reporting of what does not work. The machinery transfers; the domain is where I go to test whether it holds.

Method

The protocol I apply regardless of domain. Every project below is an instance of it, and each project page ends with the version specific to that problem.

  • Fit nothing outside the fold. Imputation, scaling and resampling belong inside each split, and metrics belong on real samples only — otherwise the score measures the pipeline leaking, not the model learning.
  • Validate on a population the model has never seen. A held-out split of the same source is not evidence of generalisation; it is evidence of interpolation.
  • Measure the shift rather than assume transfer. Stability indices and two-sample tests turn “these datasets differ” into a number you can argue about.
  • Evaluate every axis deployment depends on. Discrimination, calibration, robustness and cost dissociate — and optimising the one the field reports can actively cost you the others.
  • Correct before claiming. Friedman, then Nemenyi, then Holm. Most apparent winners do not survive contact with a multiple-comparison correction.
  • Give the model a way to abstain. Conformal and selective prediction convert silent, confident failure into a visible refusal.
  • Report what failed. Methods that did not work are evidence; omitting them is the same selective reporting that produces the problem in the first place.

Research

Leakage-free external validation for chronic kidney disease

Clinical tabularFirst author · under review, Discover Artificial Intelligence (Springer)

Imputation, scaling and oversampling re-fitted inside every cross-validation fold, with metrics computed only on real patients. Fourteen learners compared under a full statistical protocol — Friedman, Nemenyi, Holm-corrected Wilcoxon and McNemar, DeLong, bootstrap CIs — where only 6 of 78 pairwise contrasts survive correction, meaning there is no single best model. The leakage penalty grows with task difficulty, which is precisely why it stays invisible on the easy benchmark. Distribution shift quantified by Population Stability Index and a classifier two-sample test; predictive values recalibrated to deployment prevalence (PPV 100 % → 24 %); split-conformal abstention rising 14 % → 44 % out of domain. Negative results reported: TabPFN v2 does not beat tuned Extra Trees, and CTGAN/TVAE never beat no augmentation at all.

Discrimination is not reliability — a multi-axis benchmark for open-set recognition

Medical imagingSole author · under review, Int. J. of Machine Learning and Cybernetics

Twelve post-hoc scoring rules across five MedMNIST tasks and five seeds, evaluated jointly along detection, closed-set accuracy, calibration, corruption robustness and cost rather than detection alone. Friedman χ² = 77.74 with a Nemenyi critical difference of 3.376 leaves the leaders statistically tied — so the ranking everyone reports is not distinguishing them. Introduces Grad-Entropy-CAM, a gradient attribution of predictive entropy that gives a spatial rationale for rejection, validated with deletion and insertion curves rather than by eye.

Tokenization-induced miscalibration in multilingual LLMs

Multilingual NLPSole author · in progress · targeting MRL at EMNLP 2026

Asks whether subword over-fragmentation causes miscalibration — models confidently wrong precisely in the languages their tokenizer shreds — separably from data scarcity, across six Dravidian and Indic languages. The measurement phase is complete on FLORES-200 across seven tokenizers using fertility, bytes per token and Rényi efficiency. The intervention is a language-ID-free post-hoc recalibrator conditioned on the input's own fragmentation statistics, judged only on leave-one-language-out transfer to languages never seen during calibration.

Privacy-preserving federated learning for DDoS detection in 5G O-RAN

Network securityFirst author, with D. Bisen · submitted, IEEE conference

DDoSNet, a 187 KB detector sized to the memory and latency limits of a Near-RT RIC xApp, with a dependency-free packet-capture pipeline over 21 flow-level features. Trained across five federated agents on a real 5G testbed; FedProx at μ = 0.01 matches a centralised detector while every agent's data stays where it was captured.

RegimeGate — context-conditioned gating for robust demand forecasting

Demand forecastingFirst author · Winner, Indo-Swiss Research Hackathon (ETH Zürich & Lausanne)

A sub-50k-parameter gate emitting dynamic fusion weights over two frozen experts, conditioned on the observable demand regime — volatility, Syntetos–Boylan intermittency, a shock z-score — rather than on the experts' own predictions, which is what places it provably beyond a stacked meta-learner. Best overall WRMSSE 0.734 on Walmart M5 under a leakage-safe protocol, a 15.5 % improvement, and small enough to interpret with SHAP.
